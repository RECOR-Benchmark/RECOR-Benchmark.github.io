Why a new benchmark?

Existing conversational retrieval benchmarks focus on simple query reformulation, while reasoning-intensive scenarios remain underexplored. Real-world information needs often require understanding the logical flow of conversation to find relevant documents. We introduce RECOR to better benchmark retrieval on such challenging and realistic scenarios.

RECOR

RECOR is a reasoning-focused multi-turn conversational retrieval benchmark featuring 707 conversations with 2,971 turns across 11 diverse domains. It includes a Decomposition-and-Verification framework for generating grounded multi-turn dialogues with rich reasoning metadata.

RECOR Overview

What Makes RECOR Different

Reasoning-Intensive Retrieval

Queries require understanding the logical flow of conversation, building reasoning chains, and synthesizing evidence across multiple turns.

Decomposition Framework

Each conversation is generated through a Decomposition-and-Verification framework with step-wise reasoning metadata for fine-grained evaluation.

Rich Annotations

Each turn includes target information, relevance signals, and irrelevance signals to enable detailed analysis of retrieval behavior.

Domains

BRIGHT: Biology, Earth Science, Economics, Psychology, Robotics, Sustainable Living

Key Findings

Reasoning Context Doubles Performance

Combining conversation history with reasoning doubles retrieval performance (Baseline .236 â†’ History+Reasoning .479 nDCG@10, +103% improvement).

Reasoning-Specialized Models Excel

DIVER achieves .584 nDCG@10, substantially outperforming dense encoders like SFR (.464). Reasoning-enhanced approaches show consistent improvements across all 11 domains.

Turn Position Effects

Baseline performance drops 31% from Turn 1 (.324) to Turn 2 (.224). History+Reasoning shows improvement across turns, reaching .532 by Turn 4.

Small Models Competitive in RAG

7-8B parameter models score only 5-7 points below 70B+ models when provided with retrieved passages. Moderate correlation (r=.42) between retrieval and generation.
